Description

Stube2Go was done as an illustration of interaction design processes during an Interactive-Prototyping course at Köln International School of Design

Stube2Go provides an convenient way to check if the Gute Stube, the food&drinks-research-lab @ KISD is open for business. Therefor a Arduino-Ehternetboard with temperature-sensor is controlling the heat of the portafilter espressomachine while a magnetic sensor is checking if the roller door is open.

Process

We began with simple coding-excercises in processing to get a feeling for computational logic and parametrical design processes. While thinking about service-possibillities and with quick iterations we moved forward to electronical sketches, on-spot user- & product-research, model-making, paper-prototyping, investigating ethernet-protocols, building and installing a electrical prototype to sketching & coding this little web-application.

The detailed process can be seen on Instructables

Code & Tech

The core of the service is an Arduino Uno Board with Ehternet-Shield, working as a DHCP-Server providing JSON-Data of the roller door sensor & the coffee-machine controller.

Stube2Go is powered by HTML5 & JQueryMobile firing a JSONP-request to the Arduino-Board and presenting the results in an decent UI

The Code can be found on Git-Hub and is free for further use

Gute Stube is the food&drinks-research-lab @ KISD
